Android Apps for Foldable Devices:
Foldable devices have taken the market by storm. Millions of foldable devices from top brands have already been sold and more are expected to be sold. Thus, the market will continue to improve, and the Android app development company must consider that. The developers should look to make the apps compatible with all foldable devices. instant Apps:
Instant apps are not new to the market, but they have become significantly popular. Primarily, instant apps enable users to gain access to a few aspects of the app without even having to download it first. It saves users’ time and works especially well if the users need to use a one-time application. For example, having an instant app for finding cafeterias or parking lots helps users access the app whenever they need it irrespective of the fact whether they have downloaded it or not. The reason why you need to make an instant Android app is that it makes your brand more accessible to users. Besides, you also do not need to build a mobile website, which saves your time and manpower. In other words, instant apps provide you with a great user experience and visibility without spending too much cost and time.
Cloud-based Android Application:
A mobile device contains many applications. Therefore, users have become more aware of their storage space. To solve this problem, the Android app development company must make the app cloud-enabled so that users have the option of uploading a large amount of data if they need. Cloud-enabled apps improve remote collaboration, security, and all space-related issues.
Wearable Apps:
Wearable smart devices such as smartwatches are also prevalent in use. Taking the growing prominence of smartwatches into consideration, it is worthwhile to create an app compatible with such wearable devices. Robust wearable apps provide support to all kinds of smartwatches and are also portable with various sensors and GPUs.

Experts suggest that AR and VR are the future of technology. Any upcoming technology or application must have the capability to accommodate AR and VR should they become industry standards in the future.
